🌬️ Breathing Exercises for Stressful Moments

◷ 5 minute read · Published November 19, 2024 · Educational health article

Simple breathing techniques for calming the nervous system during everyday stress, without treating them as a cure-all.

Breathing exercises are popular because they are portable. You can use them before a difficult conversation, during a work break, after reading stressful news, or when you notice your shoulders rising toward your ears. They do not erase problems, but they can create a calmer starting point.

One gentle method is extended exhale breathing. Inhale through the nose for about four counts, then exhale slowly for six counts. Repeat for two to five minutes. If counting feels stressful, simply make the exhale a little longer than the inhale. Keep the breath comfortable rather than forced.

Box breathing is another option: inhale, pause, exhale, pause, using equal counts. Some people like four counts for each side of the 'box.' Others prefer shorter counts. If breath holding feels uncomfortable, skip the holds and use slow breathing instead.

Breathing works best when paired with practical action. After calming down, ask what the moment needs: a boundary, a snack, a walk, a conversation, a clearer plan, or rest. Relaxation without problem-solving can become avoidance, while action without regulation can become reactivity.

If breathing exercises trigger panic, dizziness, trauma memories, or distress, stop and use another grounding method. Professional support can help with anxiety, trauma, and panic symptoms.

Key takeaways

  • Longer exhales can help create a calm pause.
  • Keep breathing comfortable, not forced.
  • Pair regulation with practical next steps.
  • Stop if exercises increase distress.
