👀 Digital Eye Strain: Simple Screen Habits That Help

◷ 5 minute read · Published July 2, 2024 · Educational health article

Reduce eye fatigue from screens with breaks, lighting adjustments, blinking, and workstation changes.

Many people spend hours moving between laptops, phones, tablets, and televisions. Digital eye strain can feel like dry eyes, blurred vision, headaches, light sensitivity, or difficulty focusing at the end of the day. It is common, but small habits can make screen time more comfortable.

The 20-20-20 rule is a useful starting point: every 20 minutes, look at something about 20 feet away for 20 seconds. The exact numbers are less important than the pause. Your eyes need a chance to relax from close focus. Blinking also drops during screen use, which can make dryness worse.

Adjust lighting and glare. A bright window behind the screen or a harsh overhead light can increase strain. Try placing the screen slightly below eye level, increasing text size, cleaning the monitor, and using ambient light that does not reflect directly into your eyes.

If you wear glasses or contacts, make sure your prescription is current. People who use screens for work may need a specific conversation with an eye care professional about computer distance. Artificial tears may help some people with dryness, but persistent pain, sudden vision changes, or severe symptoms should be evaluated.

Screen habits are part of overall wellness. Breaks that help your eyes can also help your neck, shoulders, and attention. Stand up, stretch, and look outside when you can.

Key takeaways

  • Take regular visual breaks from close focus.
  • Reduce glare and adjust text size.
  • Blinking and hydration can affect comfort.
  • Get professional care for persistent or sudden symptoms.
